## Key Regulatory Notes (Regional Centre)

### Provincial 4-Unit As-of-Right Rule
Under NS provincial housing legislation, up to **4 dwelling units** are permitted as-of-right
on any residential lot in the urban service area. This overrides more restrictive LUB provisions
in some cases. Always flag this when a user asks about residential development in HR-1 zones.

### Parking Minimums
The Regional Centre LUB eliminated on-site parking minimums for many residential uses within
the Regional Centre, consistent with HRM Charter Minimum Planning Requirements. Always check
whether a specific zone section restores any parking requirement.

### Schmidtville Conservation District
A heritage overlay applying to parts of the South End. Has its own Bylaw (H-700) which
restricts demolition, alteration, and new construction. If the subject property is in
Schmidtville, note the overlay.

### Schedule 16 — Building Height Calculation
The Scotia Square Complex (SSC) Special Area uses a unique height calculation methodology
per Schedule 16 of the LUB.

### HAF Amendments (2024)
Housing Accelerator Fund amendments introduced new "opportunity sites" and additional
flexibility for backyard suites. These may not yet be fully reflected in the consolidated document.

## Common Regional Centre Queries at Helio

- **HR-1 zone:** Single-detached, semi-detached, secondary/backyard suites. Most restrictive
  residential zone in the Regional Centre. Watch for provincial 4-unit override.
- **HR-2 zone:** Triplex and fourplex as-of-right. Key zone for infill projects.
- **COR zone:** Corridor zone along major arterials. Allows mixed-use up to mid-rise heights.
  Converted shipping containers now permitted as housing under 2025 Regional Plan amendments.
- **ER-3 zone:** Business Park / Office employment zone. Commonly encountered in Bedford-adjacent
  areas and along Bayers Road / Burnside. Limited residential uses.
- **MU zones:** Mixed-use at varying intensities. Height limits and FAR vary by sub-zone.
